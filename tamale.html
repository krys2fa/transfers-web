TRANSFER(S): From Osnabrück to Tamale

Discursive programme at Savannah Centre for Contemporary Art (SCCA) Tamale, Ghana
November 28 - December 1, 2023

TRANSFER(S) opened on July 8th, 2023, with a monumental installation of Ibrahim Mahama’s montaged tapestry of strip-woven textiles, decommissioned jute sacks and batakaris (traditional garments cumulated from the northern region of Ghana) draping the south-west, south-east, and north-east façades of the now-closed Galeria Kaufhof building in Osnabrück’s Neumarkt district. Through Mahama’s work, the exhibition-slash-research project examines two immanent registers: 1) Osnabrück’s cottage-industrial production of textiles since the late medieval period—specifically linen with its trade and distribution to other parts of Central Europe—and its connection to the trans-Atlantic slave trade and plantation economy. And 2) the paradox of the “Peace of Westphalia”—the culmination of two peace treaties signed in the neighbouring cities of Osnabrück and Münster in 1648 which ended the devastating Thirty Years’ War in Europe. While the Westphalian system ended sectarian warfare and brought peace to Europe after a century of conflict—thereby inaugurating principles of religious tolerance, the sovereignty of member states, international diplomacy, among others—its conclusion 375 years ago also catalysed the consolidation of the European capitalist world economy and colonial expansionist project that transpired during the 17th century with consequences for our world today. Therefore, the complex leitmotifs of textile (as signifier, image, cipher, means of production, material, technology, and form of sociality) in the context of Mahama’s solo exhibition for TRANSFER(S) respond to these registers in various ways.

TRANSFER(S): From Osnabrück to Tamale, being the second phase of the project, emphasizes its research dimension with thematic interests encompassing the mercantile flows of commodities in the form of human and non-human barter objects, Christian missionisation (Lutherism, Calvininism, Zwinglianism), and colonialism with its global consequences for education, religion, economics, culture, and geopolitics. The shift from Germany to Ghana also allows for a cross-Atlantic mobility of another kind; one that comes to terms with our shared histories and affirmative futures at one and the same time. Pivoting from Osnabrück’s public space (which displayed Mahama’s site-specific installation until 1st October, 2023), SCCA Tamale will function as the discursive hub where a plurality of perspectives come together to augment the artist’s and curators’ positions. In this respect, academics, dramatists, technicians, historians, musicians, publishers, fellow artists, and curators are invited as co-workers and/or co-producers with whom to proliferate the aesthetic-historical-political strands of TRANSFER(S) which will be disseminated through roundtable talks, community-based workshops, temporary exhibitions, performances, lectures and presentations between November 28 and December 1, 2023.

TRANSFER(S) is co-curated by Kwasi Ohene-Ayeh and Bettina Klein and is under the auspices of Kunsthalle Osnabrück.

Local partners: SCCA Tamale, Red Clay, and Nkrumah Voli-Ni (all three institutions are founded by Ibrahim Mahama) and blaxTARLINES KUMASI

Profiles of Participants:

Justice Amoh
Justice Amoh is a Ghanaian artist and musician based in Kumasi. He is the founder of Koliko. Koliko is a loose ensemble of practitioners who share an interest and practice in highlife and folk music and are based in Kumasi, Ghana. For Amoh, music is a tool for entertainment and for the soul which ought to be available to everybody, irrespective of age, class, gender or racial background.

Bernard Akoi-Jackson
Bernard Akoi-Jackson (Ph.D) is an artist based in Kumasi and Accra. He is a lecturer at the Department of Painting & Sculpture at the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology in Kumasi.

Akosua Adomako Ampofo
Akosua Adomako Ampofo is Professor of African and Gender Studies at the Institute of African Studies, University of Ghana. Her work spans subjects of gender and decolonial studies, among others.

Chiara Figone
Chiara Figone is a researcher, editor and curator based in Dakar and Berlin. She is the founder and artistic director of Archive, a non-profit organization operating out of Berlin, Dakar and Milan.

Isaac Gyasi
Isaac Gyasi is currently a Principal Technician, at the Department of Indigenous Art and Technology, Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology Kumasi Ghana. He has over 21 years of professional weaving and instructional experience.

Thorsten Heese
Thorsten Heese is an historian and museum curator based in Osnabrück. He is the expert on the city’s colonial past, it’s heritage ́and conditions in the present.

Priscilla Kennedy
Priscilla Kennedy is an artist based in Kumasi, Ghana. Her work explores fabrics and processes of embroidery in relation to the human body and sexuality.

Bettina Klein
Bettina Klein is an art historian, independent curator, and author based in Berlin. She holds an M.A. in Art History and French Literature (FU Berlin, 1997). She is co-curator of TRANSFER(S).

Ibrahim Mahama
Ibrahim Mahama lives and works in Accra, Kumasi and Tamale, in Ghana. His politically-driven practice explores themes such as the contradictions inherent in economic globalization, labour relations, and creating inclusive, sustainable infrastructure in conditions of hopelessness. TRANSFER(S) (2023) is a solo exhibition of Mahama’s work.

Zohra Opoku
Zohra Opoku is a German-Ghanaian artist based in Accra, Ghana. Her work examines the politics of personal identity formation through historical, cultural, and socio-economic influences, particularly in the context of contemporary Ghana.

Kwasi Ohene-Ayeh
Kwasi Ohene-Ayeh is a curator and critic based in Kumasi, Ghana. He is co-curator of TRANSFER(S) (2023)

Gabriel Schimmeroth
Gabriel Schimmeroth is historian, curator and head of public programming at Museum am Rothenbaum Kulturen und Künste der Welt (MARKK) in Hamburg, Germany.

Anna Jehle + Juliane Schickedanz
Anna Jehle + Juliane Schickedanz are the current co-directors of the Kunsthalle Osnabrück in Germany. Kunsthalle Osnabrück is the commissioning institution for TRANSFER(S) (2023)

karî'kachä seid’ōu
karî'kachä seid’ōu is an artist-intellectual, poet, mathematician and teacher. He is Ghana's key figure in non-proprietary art and the architect of the acclaimed "Emancipatory Art Teaching Project" that transformed the fine art curriculum of the Department of Painting and Sculpture, KNUST, at the turn of the century.

Klaus Weber
Klaus Weber is a historian and Chair of European Economic and Social History, Faculty of Cultural Studies, at the European University Viadrina. His text Linen, Silver, Slaves, and Coffee: A Spatial Approach to Central Europe’s Entanglements with the Atlantic Economy! is one of the main reference texts of this project.

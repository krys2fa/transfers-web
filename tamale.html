TRANSFER(S): From Osnabrück to Tamale

Discursive programme at Savannah Centre for Contemporary Art (SCCA) Tamale, Ghana

TRANSFER(S) opened on July 8th, 2023, with a monumental installation of Ibrahim Mahama’s montaged tapestry of strip-woven textiles, decommissioned jute sacks and batakaris (traditional garments cumulated from the northern region of Ghana) draping the south-west, south-east, and north-east façades of the now-closed Galeria Kaufhof building in Osnabrück’s Neumarkt district. Through Mahama’s work, the exhibition-slash-research project examines two immanent registers: 1) Osnabrück’s cottage-industrial production of textiles since the late medieval period—specifically linen with its trade and distribution to other parts of Central Europe—and its connection to the trans-Atlantic slave trade and plantation economy. And 2) the paradox of the “Peace of Westphalia”—the culmination of two peace treaties signed in the neighbouring cities of Osnabrück and Münster in 1648 which ended the devastating Thirty Years’ War in Europe. While the Westphalian system ended sectarian warfare and brought peace to Europe after a century of conflict—thereby inaugurating principles of religious tolerance, the sovereignty of member states, international diplomacy, among others—its conclusion 375 years ago also catalysed the consolidation of the European capitalist world economy and colonial expansionist project that transpired during the 17th century with consequences for our world today. Therefore, the complex leitmotifs of textile (as signifier, image, cipher, means of production, material, technology, and form of sociality) in the context of Mahama’s solo exhibition for TRANSFER(S) respond to these registers in various ways.

TRANSFER(S): From Osnabrück to Tamale, being the second phase of the project, emphasizes its research dimension with thematic interests encompassing the mercantile flows of commodities in the form of human and non-human barter objects, Christian missionisation (Lutherism, Calvininism, Zwinglianism), and colonialism with its global consequences for education, religion, economics, culture, and geopolitics. The shift from Germany to Ghana also allows for a cross-Atlantic mobility of another kind; one that comes to terms with our shared histories and affirmative futures at one and the same time. Pivoting from Osnabrück’s public space (which displayed Mahama’s site-specific installation until 1st October, 2023), SCCA Tamale will function as the discursive hub where a plurality of perspectives come together to augment the artist’s and curators’ positions. In this respect, academics, dramatists, technicians, historians, musicians, publishers, fellow artists, and curators are invited as co-workers and/or co-producers with whom to proliferate the aesthetic-historical-political strands of TRANSFER(S) which will be disseminated through roundtable talks, community-based workshops, temporary exhibitions, performances, lectures and presentations between November 28 and December 1, 2023.

TRANSFER(S) is co-curated by Kwasi Ohene-Ayeh and Bettina Klein and is under the auspices of Kunsthalle Osnabrück.

Local partners: SCCA Tamale, Red Clay, and Nkrumah Voli-Ni (all three institutions are founded by Ibrahim Mahama) and blaxTARLINES KUMASI
